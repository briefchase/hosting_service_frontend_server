Google User Data

When you sign in with Google, we receive Google user data to authenticate you and to perform actions you request within Google Cloud. Depending on your consent, this may include your email address, name, Google Account ID, and, if granted, your phone number via the Google People API. We also obtain OAuth tokens (access token, optional refresh token, and ID token) solely to authenticate your session and call Google APIs on your behalf.

  • Scopes used include: openid, email, profile, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/compute, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/cloud-billing, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/cloudresourcemanager, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/iam, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/service.management, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/ndev.clouddomains, https://www.googleapis.com/auth/ndev.clouddns.readwrite, and optionally https://www.googleapis.com/auth/user.phonenumbers.read.
  • Tokens are stored server-side in a session with a short expiration (~20 minutes) and are not persisted beyond the session.
  • Account metadata (email, name) may be stored to operate the Service (for example, billing and support).

Sharing and Disclosure of Google User Data

We do not sell your Google user data. We share or disclose it only as described below:

  • With Google: We transmit your tokens and necessary identifiers to Google APIs strictly to provide the features you request (for example, provisioning infrastructure on Google Cloud).
  • With our billing provider (Stripe): We share your email address and an internal user identifier to create and manage your subscription. We do not share your OAuth tokens with Stripe.
  • Service operations: We may process data through our hosting and infrastructure providers acting as our processors. They are bound by contractual and technical safeguards.
  • Legal: We may disclose information if required to comply with law or to protect rights, safety, and security.

Protection of Google User Data

  • Transport security: All communications with our Service occur over HTTPS.
  • Access controls: Production systems are access-controlled and limited to authorized personnel following least-privilege practices.
  • Token handling: OAuth tokens are stored server-side in an expiring session (approximately 20 minutes) and are not retained after expiry.
  • Data minimization: We request only the scopes needed to provide the Service and use Google user data only to deliver user-requested functionality.

Retention and Deletion of Google User Data

  • Session data (including OAuth tokens and any phone number retrieved during login) is stored server-side and automatically expires after approximately 20 minutes of inactivity.
  • Account metadata (email, name) is retained as long as needed to operate the Service (e.g., subscription and support) and will be deleted upon request.
  • You may request deletion of your account data by contacting us at briefchase@googlegroups.com. After verification, we will delete retained account data, except where retention is required by law.
